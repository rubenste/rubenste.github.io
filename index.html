About this project
adidas LockerRoom is the place for teams to get all their uniform needs. Create a Locker to build your kit, add your team's names, numbers and logos and buy it from an official adidas retailer.

Challenge
The challenge for this project is that is aims to serve a wide array of users. From teams to retailers, to adidas personnel, every part of the chain is involved in the creation of new outfits, and the following retail journey. Through user testing, interviews and iterative design the MediaMonks team is working to create a seamless experience for all of these different parts.

Hartdagboek

Client-- Hartstichting
Agency -- Greenberry
Role -- Lead UX Designer
Visual Design -- Jermaine Hoebink
Find the app here

About this project
Hartstichting Hartdagboek is an app that helps people to keep track of seemingly normal symptoms, in order to have a more informed conversation with their general practitioner about the health of their heart. The basis for this analysis is a diary that asks simple questions to filter out possible risk factors.

Challenge
The Hartdagboek is aimed at a user group that is more likely to be at risk of heart disease. Our team worked with and for an older target group, and the challenge was to design with their characteristics in mind. Through thorough research and user testing, we validated a design with bigger buttons, easy interactions and helpful copy to seemlessly guide through the app and clarify where needed.

Samsung x Family Guy AR

Client-- Samsung
Agency -- MediaMonks x BBH London
Role -- UX Designer
Lead UX Designer -- Jeroen Hol

About this project
In collaboration with BBH London, MediaMonks created an AR experience to promote Samsung's intelligente home devices. The aim for this project was to make Doorways an interactive, immersive experience that shows the potential of a connected home - in a fun way.

Challenge
The challenge for this project was to make it fun for users to interact with smart devices in an AR environment. This problem we solved by creating various games that would lead visitors through challenges combining the gadgets using IoT. And apparently it worked out: our hard work was awarded with a FWA of the day.

Agency -- MediaMonks
Role -- UX Designer
Lead UX Designer -- Ben Cooper

About this project
The different fits for Calvin Klein jeans can be quite overwhelming to make sense of. My task for this project was to create the wireframes for a mobile-first experience that would create a clear overview of these fits and let visitors explore the range of models in-store â€“ exploring videos and to find that perfect pair we all search for.

Challenge
The difficulty was to design a navigation mechanic that would be both fun and logical. To reach this goal, we mapped out the 'fit grid' and defined how users might scroll through this by swiping in any direction or - in case of desktop - using the arrow keys. Great design work and smooth animations made the wireframes and grid come to life, making for a straightforward and pleasurable experience.
